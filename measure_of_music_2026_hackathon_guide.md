# Measure of Music 2026 — Hackathon-Only Guide

This document summarizes everything relevant specifically to the **hackathon/project component** of Measure of Music 2026. Conference talks, panels, and general programming are excluded unless directly relevant to project participants.

---

# 1. Hackathon Overview

Measure of Music includes a structured weekend hackathon where participants work in teams to create a **data-driven music industry project**.

### Core Objective
Build and present a functional, data-informed project within ~16–24 hours of team collaboration across 3 days.

Projects must:
- Be data-driven
- Incorporate **Chartmetric data (mandatory in 2026)**
- Be completed within the weekend
- Be presented live to judges

---

# 2. Participation Requirements

## Mandatory Confirmation
All participants must complete the mandatory confirmation form before the deadline. Failure to do so disqualifies participation.

## Daily Check-In (Mandatory)
Participants must check in daily to remain active in the hackathon.

### Check-In Deadlines
- **Day 1 (Friday):** Must check in by 5PM LA / 1AM UK
- **Day 2 (Saturday):** Check in by 10AM (latest LA time)
- **Day 3 (Sunday):** Check in by 10AM (latest LA time)

Failure to check in:
- Friday → removed from event
- Saturday → may not be replaced after cutoff
- Sunday → team may present without you

Participants must be available for the **live presentation Sunday evening**.

---

# 3. Team Formation

- Maximum ~21 teams
- Maximum ~147 project participants
- Teams assigned based on:
  - Skills
  - Experience
  - Timezone
  - Stated preferences

Participants may:
- Request to work with specific people
- Request not to work with specific people

Fully virtual teams are assigned on Day 1 after check-in.
In-person teams are assigned earlier for logistical reasons.
Hybrid teams may occur.

---

## Option B: Artist Project (Not Applicable If Not Participating)

---

# 6. Data & Tool Restrictions

Participants may use:
- Free or publicly available data
- Free trials

Participants may NOT use:
- Employer data
- University-restricted data
- Scraped data violating terms of service
- Paid-only platforms (unless free trial)

Exception:
If you legally provide equal read-only access to all participants, you may use that dataset.

# 9. Presentation Format

Each team:
- 5 minutes presentation
- 3 minutes judge Q&A
- 2 minutes transition

Three parallel rooms with separate judges.
Each room selects 1 winner.

Top 3 teams present again immediately to full audience.
Final placement determined by judges + audience vote.

All presentations are live streamed and recorded.

---

# 10. Judging Criteria

## 1. Content
- Clear objectives
- Well-explained data methodology
- Viable analysis
- Chartmetric usage included

## 2. Format
- Logical structure
- Strong visuals
- Data sources cited

## 3. Delivery
- Clear storytelling
- Engaging presentation
- Within time limit
- Good Q&A responses

## 4. Feasibility
- Real-world applicability
- Industry relevance
- Practical implementation

## 5. Implementation
- Functional report/tool/prototype built
- Evidence of actual development
- Clear development narrative

# 15. What a Strong Hackathon Project Looks Like

Strong projects typically:
- Solve a clearly defined music-industry problem
- Use Chartmetric meaningfully (not superficially)
- Combine multiple datasets intelligently
- Show a functional output (dashboard, model, prototype)
- Present 2–3 real case studies
- Have a tight narrative arc

---

# 16. Practical Reality of the Weekend

Expect:
- Fast team formation
- Time pressure
- API rate limits (Chartmetric: 4 requests/sec)
- Scope management challenges
- Final-day presentation pressure

Successful teams:
- Define scope early
- Assign clear roles
- Build something minimal but complete
- Polish storytelling on Sunday

---

# 17. Key Success Factors

- Clear problem statement
- Early data validation
- Simple but defensible methodology
- Clean visualization
- Strong narrative
- Rehearsed presentation
